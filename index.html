Homelab?

A homelab is a controlled environment designed to learn and test various computational software and hardware. Imagine it as a space where you experiment with internet-related components and explore technologies you’ve yet to try. It allows you to integrate these elements, gradually building on your knowledge and experience.

Server Rack

This piece of equipment organizes hardware, making it easily accessible for installation, troubleshooting, and hardware changes. As you can imagine, cables can get messy, so having a dedicated space to manage them makes the process much easier and more efficient.

PDU

Need more electrical outlets? Get a PDU. Whether it’s for charging your phone, laptop, powering a lamp, LED light, or your PC, a PDU has you covered. The key difference with the one shown below is that it can be rack-mounted, keeping it off the floor and neatly organized.

Patch Panel

Do you like messy cables? Probably not. That’s why adding a patch panel is a great solution for organizing them, especially Ethernet cables.

Switch

If you need more Ethernet ports to connect multiple devices, a network switch is the solution. I use a managed switch from TP-Link, which features 8 gigabit Ethernet ports for high-speed connections.

Shelf

Having a shelf in a rack is like having a regular shelf in your kitchen—you can store whatever you need. It’s perfect for simple storage or housing non-rackmountable hardware.

Server Rails

To securely hold our server, we need racks to support it. While screwing it directly into place might work, it would put too much pressure on the screws due to the server's length, leading to uneven weight distribution.
