Mistake #1

When you are making patch cables,you have to make sure that the cables are flush/equal when you plug it into "loT"(Internet of Things) such as a switch or router. If it's not straight, the connection will not go through. To fix this, use a crimp and cutter tool. This tool makes sure that the cables are flush but also crimps it, keeping the cables in place.

Mistake #2

As we can see, the rails to hold the server are unstable. This causes issues as it can eventually fall. I only used screws to put it in,which is not the correct way. Unfortunately, my rack cannot use rack studs, therefore, I opted for screws and a washer. suprisingly, a washer distributes the load, stress of fastening point, and maintains tightness. If you are buying a rack, make sure it fits 19 inch networking equipment and uses rack studs.
